How it works

01

Your photo is decoded straight into a canvas in your browser. It's never sent to a server.

02

Dragging the quality and dimension dials re-renders the canvas and re-estimates the file size live.

03

When you download, the browser exports the canvas as a real file at the size shown. That's it.

Common questions

Is my photo uploaded anywhere?

No. Everything happens locally using the HTML canvas API. Nothing is sent to a server, which is also why this tool works offline once the page has loaded.

Why is my output larger than expected?

PNG doesn't support a quality slider the way JPG and WebP do — it only shrinks when you lower the max dimension. For the smallest file at a given quality, use JPG or WebP.

What's a good size for a passport photo or form upload?

Most government and visa forms ask for under 100KB or 200KB. Try quality around 70% and a max dimension of 800–1000px, then adjust the dials until the estimate lands under the limit.
